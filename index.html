Research Interest

Physics seeks to uncover the universal properties of the natural world and to truly understand them. My focus has been on systems with many (often infinitely many) degrees of freedom, where rich and unexpected phenomena emerge. I have worked across statistical physics, condensed matter physics, and field theory, aiming to establish mathematically rigorous results that also carry genuine insight for physicists. Recent topics include:
  • Low-energy properties of quantum spin systems, expecially nontrivial Symmetry-protected topological (SPT) phases in spin chains
  • Thermalization in isolated macroscopic quantum systems
  • Characterization of "quantum chaotic" natures of some concrete (but simple and standard) quantum many-body system
  • Universal relations in nonequilibrium statistical mechanics
